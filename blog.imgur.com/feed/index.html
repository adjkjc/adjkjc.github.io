Despite the ongoing (at the time of writing) COVID-19 lockdown, Imgurians have been striking out on a virtual field trip of the universe, sharing their past travels and experiences with the Imgur Community for the whole week of April 27 to May 3.

With over 3,100 submissions to the Field Trip tag, it has become a veritable cornucopia of interesting places, from arid deserts to abandoned theme parks to monster truck rallies.

Join us as we take a look at a few of our favorites! Or if you’d like to check them all out for yourself, take a look at the tag.

Abandoned six flags

Silently rusting on the outskirts of New Orleans, Louisiana and sprawling 140-acres is the once-bustling Six Flags New Orleans theme park. Initially closed temporarily due to Hurricane Katrina back in 2005, the park suffered severe salt-water flooding and remained submerged for many months, sadly resulting in its abandonment and, ultimately, sometime in the near future – its demolition.

Imgurian @nawwwwson took us on a tour of the eerie park, complete with rides, creepy graffiti, and empty streets.

Great Lakes Science and Sailing

If you grew up in North America you’re no doubt very familiar with the Great Lakes, vast bodies of water spanning between the United States and Canada that are best compared with inland oceans. For centuries these freshwater behemoths have been a backbone of trade, with thousands of ships of all sizes plying the coasts, locks, levies, and ports of the five lakes.

Imgurian readingrabbit took us aboard an 850ft three-Masted Cargo Schooner from the 1850s on an adventure and explains the intricacies of each lake, the trade plied on them, and even explores a few shipwrecks on the way.

City Museum, St. Louis, MO — The Most Proudly Unsafe Museum EVER

No field trip is complete without a stop at a museum, which is exactly where @StepRightUp took us. Welcome to the City Museum, in St. Louis, Missouri, a unique half-museum-half-art-exhibit that resides in the heart of downtown STL.

From crawling through caves to climbing out on the wing of an aircraft, this unique post offered a fantastic glimpse at the ever-evolving museum through the eyes of an Imgurian.

Let's go on a field trip to Monster Jam! Because I'm a grown woman who still enjoys monster trucks.

This Sunday! Sunday! Sunday!

In a post that’s sure to tickle any motor-head, our very own @sarah took us on a trip to a monster-truck rally in Oakland, California. Walking us through the intricacies and nuances of monster-trucking, audience votes, and sick truck flips.

One thing is for certain, with this post you pay for the full seat, but you’ll only need the edge!

Antarctica – Bucket list ✓

Few places are more remote, desolate, and inhospitable as the Antarctic. Setting off from South America, Imgurian @xEmpressDee took us along on her family’s bucket list expedition. Featuring penguins, seasickness, and adorable seals, this incredible post took us to a place few of us will ever get the chance to venture.

We hope you enjoyed going on the first-ever Imgur Field Trip as much as we did! Don’t forget to check out the tag if you’d like to see more, and thanks to all the Imgurians who submitted their wonderful adventures.

]]> https://blog.imgur.com/2020/05/05/the-imgur-field-trip-2020-roundup/feed/ 0 COVID-19: How Social Distancing Has Created New Digital Campfires & Ways Your Brand Can Get Involved https://blog.imgur.com/2020/04/07/covid-19-how-social-distancing-has-created-new-digital-campfires/ https://blog.imgur.com/2020/04/07/covid-19-how-social-distancing-has-created-new-digital-campfires/#comments Tue, 07 Apr 2020 16:31:45 +0000 http://blog.imgur.com/?p=12891 By Catherine Chang (Senior Integrated Marketing Manager), Michael Peroutka (VP of Brand Partnerships) & Jimmy Marsanico (Head of Data) 

As COVID-19 continues to keep people away from family and friends, at least physically, entertainment and social platforms across the board have been bombarded with an unprecedented surge in usage. Over here at Imgur, we’ve seen over double digit growth in traffic over the past week compared to our 12 week running average.

We are now at over 300 million monthly unique visitors and 2.8 billion page views globally. We believe that these increases are due to a variety of factors, including:

  1. Imgur’s Positive Tone – As Imgur’s mission is to lift the world’s spirits for a few moments a day, we’ve seen a strong uptick in traffic over the past week as people are looking for moments of bite-sized entertainment that puts a smile on their face (like this) to combat everything they are seeing in the news and on other social platforms. As we’ve proven out through research with YPulse, Imgur is one of the few platforms that drives happiness amongst the majority of its users.
  2. People Working From Home – With commute times and the fear of co-workers catching you browning the internet eliminated, more people are visiting Imgur between the hours of 8 AM – 6 PM than ever before!  
  3. Cancellation of Sporting Events – With 73% of our audience falling within the A18-44 demographic, we compete with the likes of ESPN, Bleacher Report, etc. for attention. As those platforms don’t have much to report on, consumers are turning their attention elsewhere.  

Millennials and Gen Z Favor Digital Campfires Over Traditional Social Media as the Pandemic Disrupts Normal Life

What we find even more interesting is that the pandemic has proven that the shift from traditional social media platforms to “digital campfires” is no longer an emerging trend. It has arrived. As millennials and Gen Z continue to reshape their relationship with social media, they are craving more authentic experiences instead of carefully curated online personas.

We can pinpoint one of the reasons behind this shift to influencer fatigue. After years of being bombarded by perfectly manicured imagery and messaging, younger consumers are savvier than ever and can easily see when an influencer is behaving in a way that feels disingenuous. 

They’ve also increasingly turned to online communities that can provide them with a refuge from coworkers and the prying eyes of online “friends” they’ve accumulated over time, family included. These communities, also known as digital campfires, are more intimate online gatherings based on shared interests, allowing people to connect with like-minded people without the scrutiny or judgement that often comes with traditional social media platforms. 

Digital campfires can be private messaging, micro-communities, shared experiences or a combination of all three, as illustrated in the examples below.

  • Facebook is seeing an extreme spike in usage across the globe because of the pandemic, but interestingly, most usage is concentrated in private messaging and video calling. They have seen messaging increase over 50% and time in group calling increase by over 1,000% during the last month.
  • Discord, a 1-on-1 and group text and voice messaging app popular with teens and the video gaming community, has gone mainstream on a whole new level. In fact, demand has been so explosive that the app crashed several times in the past week. Discord responded by increasing its overall server capacity and temporarily increasing the cap on the number of people who can join a live screen-sharing channel from 10 to 50. 
  • Imgur has seen double digit growth in post views in the past week. Now more than ever, people are looking to escape the relentless news cycle while searching for new ways to entertain themselves. At Imgur, they can find digital campfires that can help them learn something, feel inspired, or simply laugh a little as their attention turns inward and indoors. 

What Are the Most Active Digital Campfires on Imgur Right Now?

Isolation doesn’t need to be isolating when you can participate in a community that shares the same interests as you. The birth of #DistancingTogether on Imgur supports our belief that the human desire to connect with others, away from influencers and the throngs of “friends” on traditional social media, has only grown stronger during this time.

Popular Digital Campfires on Imgur in the Past 2 Weeks

Streaming

Being stuck at home combined with the demise of linear sports TV viewing for the season has turned people to alternative sources of entertainment such as Imgur, where a wealth of digital campfires have popped up, covering topics as broad as sharing #WorkFromHome setups to #TigerKing memes that might only be funny to those who are in the know.

TV analytics company EDO surveyed 6,809 people in the US from March 24 to March 28 about their viewing habits. 85% of those who responded said that their at-home TV and movie watching has increased recently. The clear winners according to the survey were Disney plus, followed by Hulu and then Netflix.

Percent of EDO Survey Respondents Who Subscribed To New Streaming Services While Social Distancing

With jokes abound about what the heck to watch next, Imgur users are helping each other out by providing suggestions in #MoviesToWatch.

Gaming

Americans are gaming a lot more as it provides a new normal of fun and fellowship. Verizon reported that U.S. video game usage during peak hours has gone up 75% since the quarantine first went into effect. This means that people are not only sharing their work from home setups, they are also showing off their gaming rigs (#IsolationStation).

We are seeing a resurgence of #AnimalCrossing conversations as the latest addition to the beloved gaming franchise smashed sales records after launching on March 20th. The game’s appeal is apparent during a time when the real world can seem unpredictable and anxiety-ridden as it transports players to an idyllic island while providing structure and a sense of purpose through daily tasks like fishing and gardening.

The new #Doom game, which launched on the same day as Animal Crossing, also benefited from the massive uptick in gaming.

DIY

We’re also seeing people taking this time away from other people as an opportunity to learn a new skill or take a deeper dive into their hobbies, whether that’s cooking (#SavorySolitude #ImgurianCookbook) or crafting (#HomeHobbyist).

Amusing

As the novelty of shelter-in-place wore off, we discovered that people not only leaned heavily on their favorite internet pastimes on Imgur, such as #OddlySatisfying, they also started to come up with new ways to stave off boredom. Take #StayatHomeGames for example, where people shared how they juggled rings or tested the limits of their family bond by playing Monopoly on not just one board but on two boards simultaneously.

Additionally, we discovered that people are very interested in rallying around events that may seem silly but ultimately give them a sense of belonging to the Imgur community. Our annual April Fool’s prank this year was inspired by our community’s inside joke, known as “#BananaforScale.” We implemented a banana button that allowed users to measure posts on Imgur on a sliding banana scale. 

In response, Imgur users shared banana art, created banana GIFs, and even showed off a picture of their new puppy next to a banana. With over 1 million clicks on the banana button in the span of a day, it’s clear that sharing a laugh over something that might only be funny to a specific group of people can be a source of powerful connection during a time of crisis.

As a Marketer, How Can Your Brand Participate?

The spread of COVID-19 has presented humanity with an unprecedented challenge on a global scale. In an era when Millennials and Gen Z are already suspicious about the motives of large brands, it can seem like the best foot forward is to stay quiet during this time. 

Research has shown otherwise. On March 16th, IPSOS interviewed 999 adults aged 18-65 through a poll, and the results overwhelmingly pointed to people’s desire to hear from helpful brands. 74% of respondents agreed with the following statement: “I want to continue to hear from brands and companies on what they have to offer.”

Brands now have a unique opportunity to step up and interact with people in a meaningful way. Here are some communication strategies that we’ve come up with to help brands who are struggling with how to engage their customers right now.

  1. Create and distribute content that provides value. 

First and foremost, people want to know what they and their loved ones need to stay safe and healthy. In this time of uncertainty, people are also looking for guidance and a sense of reassurance as they adjust to their new normal. In the same poll created by IPSOS, 71% of respondents said that they are interested in hearing from brands that can help them navigate the crisis. It’s also important to note here that we’ve found that 69% of the top performing pieces of branded content on Imgur in 2019 were informational in nature. Here is an example of a post we created with tips on how to stay active mentally and physically while social distancing. 

  1. Foster and facilitate digital campfires that help people stay connected and entertained. 

Membership within digital campfires is built around a common interest or pastime. One straightforward way for brands to join or even start a digital campfire is to host a virtual event that is of interest to the audience they are trying to reach. We believe that people are looking for fun and interesting things to do as a respite from their new reality. 

For example, brands have successfully engaged Imgur’s meme-loving Millennial and Gen Z audience with the GIF Creator Challenge, where a select number of Imgur power users are invited to create GIFs that tap into timely events or trends. Imgur users at large are then invited to participate by voting on their favorite GIFs. 

  1. Empower people to give back. 

News about COVID-19 may leave some people feeling helpless. To combat this, brands can step up to inspire positivity and change. For example, last year, Mike’s Hard Lemonade invited Imgur users to share stories about their families and helped 2 of them fly home to surprise their parents, including a veteran who had not seen his mother in years. 

Another example is Tito’s Handmade Vodka, which partnered with Imgur to award $5,000 every quarter to a special person who will use it to make a difference in their community. Brands that have given back to their communities or given people the means to do so have had resounding success on Imgur in the past.

While it may seem daunting for brands to step up to the plate during this time, data shows that people expect brands to stay active. With the right creative and messaging, brands can earn trust and confidence from people who need their products and services. Reach out to the Imgur team for help crafting brand stories that will help lift people’s spirits during this challenging time.

Hide-the-pain-Harold
