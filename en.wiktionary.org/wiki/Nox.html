English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin Nox.

Proper noun[edit]

Nox

  1. (Roman mythology) The Roman goddess of night.

Anagrams[edit]

  • XON

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /noks/, [nɔks]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /noks/, [nɔks]

Proper noun[edit]

Nox f sg (genitive Noctis); third declension

  1. Night (personification)
  2. The goddess Nox

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun, singular only.
