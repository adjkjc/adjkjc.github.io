Old English[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • gōdnys

Etymology[edit]

From gōd +‎ -nes. Cognate with Old High German gōtnassī, cōtnassī (“goodness”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɡoːd.nes/

Noun[edit]

gōdnes f (nominative plural gōdnessa)

  1. the quality or condition of being good; goodness

Descendants[edit]

  • Middle English: godnesse, goodnesse
    • English: goodness
