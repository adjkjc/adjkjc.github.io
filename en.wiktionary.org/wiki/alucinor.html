Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • hālūcinor
  • hallucinor

Etymology[edit]

Possibly from Ancient Greek ἀλύω (alúō, “to wander in mind, to roam”), with influence from other -cinor verbs. See ambulō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /aːˈluː.ki.nor/, [aːˈɫ̪uː.kɪ.nɔr]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /aˈlu.t͡ʃi.nor/, [aˈluː.t͡ʃi.nɔr]

Verb[edit]

ālūcinor (present infinitive ālūcinārī, perfect active ālūcinātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. I wander in mind, talk idly, prate, dream.
