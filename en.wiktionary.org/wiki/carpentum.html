Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Gaulish carbantos, from Proto-Celtic *karbantos (“chariot, war chariot”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /karˈpen.tum/, [karˈpɛn̪.t̪ʊ̃ˑ]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /karˈpen.tum/, [karˈpɛn̪.t̪um]

Noun[edit]

carpentum n (genitive carpentī); second declension

  1. carriage (two-wheeled); chariot
  2. wagon, cart
  3. barouche

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
