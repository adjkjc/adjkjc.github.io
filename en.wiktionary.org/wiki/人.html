Sagart (1999) relates it to Tibetan ཉེན (nyen, “relative”), from ཉེ (nye, “near”) + nominalizing suffix -n; if so, from Proto-Sino-Tibetan *s-naːj ~ *s-nej (“near”).

Schuessler (2007) proposes two etymologies:

  • Same etymon as 仁 (OC *njin, “to be kind; to be good”), from Proto-Sino-Tibetan *s/k-niŋ (“heart; brain; mind”). The semantic association of 仁 with 人 was somewhat late, dated to Mencius's time; later on, 仁 would be usually interpreted as "humane, acting like a human being".
    • If so, semantically parallel with Tibetan སེམས་ཅན (sems can, “living being, human, animal”, literally “possessor of mind”); and Latin animalis (“living, having soul”) from anima (“soul”).
  • Related to Proto-Mon-Khmer *ɲaʔ ~ *ɲah (“person”) with a nominalizing suffix -n, from the auto-ethnonym of a hostile, possibly Austroasiatic-speaking, eastern people, whom the Sinitic-speaking Shang referred to as 人方 (OC *njin paŋ).

    Definitions[edit]

    人

    1. man; person; people (Classifier: 個／个 m c; 隻／只 h)
      • 那人是誰？ / 那人是谁？  ―  Nà rén shì shuí?  ―  Who is that person?
      • 嗰個人係邊個嚟㗎？ [Cantonese, trad.]
        𠮶个人系边个嚟㗎？ [Cantonese, simp.]
        Go2 go3 jan4-2 hai6 bin1 go3 lai4 gaa3? [Jyutping]
        Who is that person?
      • 有人敲門了。 / 有人敲门了。  ―  Yǒurén qiāomén le.  ―  Someone knocked on the door.
    2. human being (Homo sapiens)
      • 人類 / 人类  ―  rénlèi  ―  human
    3. body
      • 這兩天人不大舒服。 [MSC, trad.]
        这两天人不大舒服。 [MSC, simp.]
        Zhè liǎng tiān rén bùdà shūfú. [Pinyin]
        I [lit. the body] haven't been feeling that well these few days.
    4. This term needs a translation to English. Please help out and add a translation, then remove the text {{rfdef}}.
      • 今天上午你人在哪？ [MSC, trad. and simp.]
        Jīntiān shàngwǔ nǐ rén zài nǎ? [Pinyin]
        Where have you been this morning?
    5. everybody; everyone
      • 人人  ―  rénrén  ―  everybody
      • 人手一冊 / 人手一册  ―  rénshǒu yīcè  ―  It's a bestseller. (lit. Everyone has a copy.)
    6. physical, psychological or moral quality or condition; character; personality
      • 就我所知，他人不錯。 [MSC, trad.]
        就我所知，他人不错。 [MSC, simp.]
        Jiùwǒsuǒzhī, tā rén bùcuò. [Pinyin]
        As far as I know, he is kind (lit. his personality is not bad).
      • 我不知道他是什麼人。 [MSC, trad.]
        我不知道他是什么人。 [MSC, simp.]
        Wǒ bùzhīdào tā shì shénme rén. [Pinyin]
        I don't know what kind of person he is.
      • 佢到底係咩人嚟㗎？ [Cantonese, trad.]
        佢到底系咩人嚟㗎？ [Cantonese, simp.]
        Keoi5 dou3 dai2 hai6 me1 jan4 lai4 gaa3? [Jyutping]
        Exactly what kind of person is he?
    7. a person associated with a particular identity or trait; -er
      • 主持人  ―  zhǔchírén  ―  host
      • 香港人 [Cantonese]  ―  hoeng1 gong2 jan4 [Jyutping]  ―  Hongkonger
    8. manpower; worker; employee
    9. † talent; person of talent
    10. † other people; others
      • 人云亦云  ―  rényún yìyún  ―  to follow the crowd (lit. to say what others say)
    11. † common people; commoner
    12. † adult; grown-up
      • 長大成人 / 长大成人  ―  chángdà chéngrén  ―  to grow up to be an adult
    13. † reason; cause
    14. † sexual intercourse
      • 人道  ―  réndào  ―  sexual intercourse
      • 人事  ―  rénshì  ―  sexual intercourse
    15. † company; companion; friend
    16. † artificial; man-made
      • 人禍 / 人祸  ―  rénhuò  ―  human disaster
    17. † mortal; bodily; earthly
      • 人間 / 人间  ―  rénjiān  ―  the mortal world
    18. † Alternative form of 仁 (rén, “kernel”).
    19. † Alternative form of 仁 (rén, “charity”).
    20. † Alternative form of 仁 (rén, “loving; kind”).
    21. (Beijing) Lenition of 人家 (rénjia).
      • 別磨蹭啦，人在外頭等著我們呢！ [dialectal Mandarin, trad.]
        别磨蹭啦，人在外头等着我们呢！ [dialectal Mandarin, simp.]
        Bié móceng la, rén zǎi wàitou děng zhe wǒmen ne! [Pinyin]
        Stop dilly-dallying. He/She/They is/are waiting for us outside!
    22. (Taishanese) paternal grandmother
