English[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Rhymes: -eɪtəl

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Latin nātālis (“natal”), from nātus, perfect active participle of nāscor (“I am born”), from gnāscor, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵenh₁-.

Adjective[edit]

natal

  1. Of or relating to birth.
Related terms[edit]
► English terms derived from the PIE root *ǵenh₁-‎ (0 c, 83 e)
Translations[edit]
Of or relating to birth
  • Finnish: syntymä-
  • Portuguese: natal (pt)
  • Romanian: natal (ro), de naștere

Etymology 2[edit]

From Latin natis (“rump”), plural nates.

Adjective[edit]

natal (comparative more natal, superlative most natal)

  1. Of or relating to the buttocks.
Related terms[edit]
  • nates
  • natiform
Translations[edit]
Of or relating to the buttocks

Further reading[edit]

  • natal in Webster’s Revised Unabridged Dictionary, G. & C. Merriam, 1913.
  • natal in The Century Dictionary, The Century Co., New York, 1911

Anagrams[edit]

  • alant

Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin natalis. Doublet of Nadal.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Balearic, Central) IPA(key): /nəˈtal/
  • (Valencian) IPA(key): /naˈtal/

Adjective[edit]

natal (masculine and feminine plural natals)

  1. natal

French[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin nātālis. Doublet of Noël.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /na.tal/
  • audio
    		(file)

Adjective[edit]

natal (feminine singular natale, masculine plural nataux, feminine plural natales)

  1. native
    ville natale — home town

Further reading[edit]

  • “natal” in Trésor de la langue française informatisé (The Digitized Treasury of the French Language).

Norwegian Nynorsk[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin natalis.

Adjective[edit]

natal (masculine and feminine natal, neuter natalt, definite singular and plural natale, comparative natalare, indefinite superlative natalast, definite superlative natalaste)

  1. pertaining to birth

References[edit]

  • “natal” in The Nynorsk Dictionary.

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin natalis. Doublet of Nadal.

Adjective[edit]

natal m or f (plural natais, comparable)

  1. natal (of or relating to birth)
  2. native (relating to the place where one was born)

Synonyms[edit]

  • (natal): natalício

Derived terms[edit]

  • terra natal
  • país natal

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin nātālis (“natal”). Compare also the doublet nadal.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /naˈtal/, [naˈt̪al]

Adjective[edit]

natal (plural natales)

  1. natal
  2. native
    país natal — native country
  3. home
