Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From videō (“I see”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈwiː.sus/, [ˈwiː.sʊs]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈvi.sus/, [ˈviː.zus]

Noun[edit]

vīsus m (genitive vīsūs); fourth declension

  1. The action of looking.
  2. The power of sight.
  3. A vision, sight, apparition.
  4. Appearance

Declension[edit]

Fourth-declension noun.

  • videō

Descendants[edit]

  • English: advice, vision
  • French: avis, visage
  • Galician: viso
  • Istriot: veîso
  • Italian: viso, visivo
  • Portuguese: aviso, viso
  • Spanish: aviso, viso, visiva, visura

Participle[edit]

vīsus (feminine vīsa, neuter vīsum); first/second-declension participle

  1. having been seen
  2. having been looked (at)
  3. having been observed
  4. having been understood
