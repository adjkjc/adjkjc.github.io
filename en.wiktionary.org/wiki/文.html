Ideogram (指事): a man with a painted or tattooed chest – original form of 紋. In current form, tattoo has vanished, so simply an outline of a man (head, outstretched arms, legs, chest). Compare 大, which has no chest.

    Definitions[edit]

    文

    1. † Original form of 紋／纹 (wén, “mark, pattern, design, tattoo, decoration”).
      文如貍 / 文如狸 [Classical Chinese]  ―  wén rú lí [Pinyin]  ―  having a pattern like that of a wild cat
    2. Original form of 紋／纹 (wén, “to tattoo”).
      文身  ―  wénshēn  ―  to tattoo
      文面  ―  wénmiàn  ―  face tattoo
    3. writing; literary composition; literature (Classifier: 篇 c)
      文件  ―  wénjiàn  ―  document
      文章  ―  wénzhāng  ―  article, essay
      呢篇文寫得唔錯。 [Cantonese, trad.]
      呢篇文写得唔错。 [Cantonese, simp.]
      Ni1 pin1 man4-2 se2 dak1 m4 co3. [Jyutping]
      This essay is written quite well.
    4. literary
    5. Short for 文讀／文读 (wéndú, “literary reading”).
    6. script; written form
      甲骨文  ―  jiǎgǔwén  ―  oracle bone script
      書同文，車同軌 / 书同文，车同轨 [Classical Chinese]  ―  shū tóng wén, chē tóng guǐ [Pinyin]  ―  (please add an English translation of this example)
      天城文  ―  tiānchéngwén  ―  Devanagari
    7. (written) language
      Antonym: 語／语 (yǔ)
      中文  ―  zhōngwén  ―  Chinese
      英文  ―  yīngwén  ―  English
    8. culture
      文化  ―  wénhuà  ―  culture
      文明  ―  wénmíng  ―  civilization
    9. of a government position, non-military: civil, civilian
      文職 / 文职  ―  wénzhí  ―  civilian post
      文官  ―  wénguān  ―  civil official
      文武  ―  wénwǔ  ―  civilian and military
    10. refined; educated; cultured; intellectual
      斯文  ―  sīwén  ―  refined, cultured
      文雅  ―  wényǎ  ―  elegant, refined
      文人  ―  wénrén  ―  scholar
    11. 47th tetragram of the Taixuanjing; "pattern, repetition" (𝌴)
    12. (archaic) Classifier for coins.
    13. (numismatics) mill (a tenth of a cent)
      一文  ―  yī wén  ―  one mill
    14. A surname​.

    Etymology 1[edit]

    Kanji in this term
    文
    もん
    Grade: 1
    on’yomi

    From Middle Chinese 文 (mjun, “ornate; a kind of coin; language, literature, a letter, a character”).

    Pronunciation[edit]

    • On’yomi: Goon
      • (Tokyo) も​ん [móꜜǹ] (Atamadaka – [1])[1][2]
      • IPA(key): [mõ̞ɴ]

    Noun[edit]

    文(もん) • (mon) 

    1. the mon, an old currency
    2. a unit of length for measuring the size of one's foot (from the way that the mon coins would be lined up and used as a kind of inch marker)
    3. a character, a letter
    4. writing, something written
    5. a magical spell
    6. (also spelled 紋) a design, a pattern
    7. (also spelled 紋) a 家紋 (kamon, “family crest”)
    Usage notes[edit]

    The design and crest senses are more often spelled 紋.

    Etymology 3[edit]

    Kanji in this term
    文
    ふみ
    Grade: 1
    kun’yomi

    Possibly a shift from an older Middle Chinese-derived reading pun.[3] Earlier Japanese writing was ambiguous regarding the final n sound spelled ん in modern Japanese, with this sound often spelled (and possibly pronounced) as む (mu) instead.

    */pumu/ → /pumi/ → /ɸumi/

    This reading appears in the Heian period, in works such as The Tale of Genji.[3][1]

    Pronunciation[edit]

    • Kun’yomi
      • (Tokyo) ふ​み [fúꜜmì] (Atamadaka – [1])[1][2]
      • IPA(key): [ɸɯ̟ᵝmʲi]

    Noun[edit]

    文(ふみ) • (fumi) 

    1. writing, something written
    2. a letter (written communication), an epistle
    3. (archaic) study, scholarship (especially of classical Chinese)
    4. a style of 家紋 (kamon, “family crest”)
    Synonyms[edit]
    • (letter, epistle): 手(て)紙(がみ) (tegami)
    Derived terms[edit]
    • 恋(こい)文(ぶみ) (koibumi): a love letter

    Etymology 4[edit]

    Kanji in this term
    文
    あや
    Grade: 1
    kun’yomi

    Uncertain. Appears in texts from the early Heian period.[3][1]

    Pronunciation[edit]

    • Kun’yomi
      • (Tokyo) あや​ [àyáꜜ] (Odaka – [2])[1][2]
      • IPA(key): [a̠ja̠]

    Noun[edit]

    文(あや) • (aya) 

    1. (also spelled 紋, 綾)
      1. a pattern, a design, especially one with diagonally crossing lines
      2. a diagonal crossing
      3. a flowery expression or turn of phrase
      4. a plot, a scheme
      5. a refrain or recurring phrase in a piece of music
      6. a harder vein or grain in a leaf or piece of wood or bamboo
      7. a stain, a blemish
      8. a mediator, a go-between (apparently from the way that a go-between can make the relationship more indirect and indistinct)
      9. a small indistinct fluctuation in a trend line
    2. (also spelled 綾, 絢)
      1. a kind of woven silk textile with a diagonally crossing pattern
      2. short for 綾織 (ayaori): a twill weave; someone who weaves twill
      3. short for 綾竹 (ayadake): in a loom, a bamboo pole placed between the heddle and the back beam or platen, used to improve the handling
      4. short for 綾取り (ayatori): cat's cradle
    Usage notes[edit]

    For the second group of senses, the 綾 spelling is more common.

    Derived terms[edit]
    Derived terms
    • 奇(あや) (aya), 怪(あや) (aya): mystifying, mysterious, strange (from the way that an aya pattern could confound the eyes)
    • 綾(あや)織(おり) (ayaori): a twill weave; someone who weaves twill
    • 綾(あや)竹(だけ) (ayadake): in a loom, a bamboo pole placed between the heddle and the back-beam or platen, used to improve the handling
    • 綾(あや)取(と)り (ayatori): cat's cradle
