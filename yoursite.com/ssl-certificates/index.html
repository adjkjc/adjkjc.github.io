    How Does SSL Certificates Work?

    THE SSL HANDSHAKE

    When a browser tries to access a website that is secured by SSL, a connection is established between the browser and the web domain by a process called the “SSL Handshake”. This is an invisible connection and happens very quickly.

    Encrypting and decrypting with private and public key takes a lot of processing power and thus, they are only used during the SSL Handshake to create a session key.

    After the secure connection is created, the session key is used to encrypt all transmitted data.

    SSL CERTIFICATE KEYS

    Basically, there are three keys that are used to set up the SSL connection: the public, private, and session keys. Any encryption with the public key can only be decrypted with the private key, and vice versa.

    This type of cryptography harnesses the power of two keys which are a series of randomly generated numbers. The public key is known to your server and is available within the public domain. So, it’s important that the other keys remains private.

    SSL Certificate Frequently Asked Questions - FAQ

    What does SSL mean?

    SSL certificate means Secure Socket Layer certificate and it is a document used to prove that an individual or organization is who they say they are. The certificate shows the public the true name of the company and the full web domain name that the certificate is registered.

    The secure certificate transmits data on the SSL which then encrypts the data using decryption and encryption technology. This is a key component for online businesses because it creates a trusted environment for potential customers to feel confident while making purchases.

    SSL certificates establish a secure connection thus establishing trust and authentication.

    What are SSL certificates used for?

    SSL certificates provide tough encryption for data transferred from server to browser. The procedure or obtaining an SSL for your website is different depending on your choice of SSL certificate. Registered domains are for public use, one needs to comply with the rules regulated by the Certificate Authorities (CA) which are used to obtain SSL certificates.

    Which SSL certificate is right for my website?

    Once you own a website, you’ll need a trust bearing SSL certificate. Finding the exact SSL certificate type for your needs depends on the number of domains you want to secure, the registration validity of the certificate, and the level of security you require.

    1. How many domains do you want to protect?
    If you have a single domain website, then our standard Domain Validated (DV) SSL will do the trick. This SSL certificate price tag is at the lowest tier but still offers strong server to browser encryption.

    Wildcards and multi-domain certificates can also be used if you want protection for numerous domains. Select our PositiveSSL Wildcard DV if you have one main domain and several sub domain pages to secure. For more than one domain names, a wise decision will be to choose a PositiveSSL multi-domain (DV). It is more favorable to use a multi-domain certificate because it gives more security at a cheaper price.

    Imagine having to purchase a certificate for every domain. Save money and time with wildcard and multi-domain certificates.

    2. Do you own a business website? 
    If you just operate a personal website our basic Positive DV SSL is perfect! However once you handle personal data and transactions on your site we suggest a Multi-domain SSL or our Sectigo EV SSL for the toughest encryption and trust levels.

    How long does it take to get an SSL Certification?

    To time it takes to get an SSL certificate depends on the security level your website requires.

    Domain Validation (DV) takes at most one day.

    Organization Validation (OV) takes 1-2 days to authenticate including four verification processes; which are a voice call, company authentication, and domain and locality verification.

    Extended Validation (EV) certificates take anything between 3-5 days because of the longer verification requirements.

    What basic information can you find on an SSL certificate?

    An SSL certificates document bears the creation and expiration dates, a copy of the certificate holder’s public key and the digital signature of the certificate (the issuing SSL certificate authority).
