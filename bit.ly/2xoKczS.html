Faroese[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [erʊ]

Verb[edit]

eru

  1. first/second/third-person plural present of vera (“to be”): are
    • vit eru føroyingar
      we are Faroese
    • eru tit eingilsmenn?
      are you Englishmen?
    • tey eru glað
      they are happy
    • tær eru tríggjar konur
      they are three women
    • teir eru fimm menn
      they are five men
    • tygum eru ov seint
      you (formal) are too late
    • eru tygum ...?
      are you ...?

Conjugation[edit]

Icelandic[edit]

Verb[edit]

eru

  1. third-person plural present of vera (“to be”): they are
    Menn eru illir.
    Men are evil.

Japanese[edit]

Romanization[edit]

eru

  1. Rōmaji transcription of える

Malay[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • ru
  • ارو‎
  • رو‎

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Malayic *(h)Aru, from Proto-Malayo-Chamic *(h)aru, from Proto-Malayo-Sumbawan *(h)aru, from Proto-Sunda-Sulawesi *(h)aru, from Proto-Malayo-Polynesian *(q)aʀuhu.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /əru/
  • Rhymes: -əru, -ru, -u

Noun[edit]

eru (Jawi spelling ارو)

  1. casuarina (trees of the genus Casuarina)

Synonyms[edit]

  • cemara / چمارا‎

Palauan[edit]

Palauan cardinal numbers
 <  1 2 3  > 
    Cardinal : eru

Etymology[edit]

From Pre-Palauan *ruwa, from Proto-Malayo-Polynesian *duha, from Proto-Austronesian *duSa.

Numeral[edit]

eru

  1. two

Westrobothnian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

ér +‎ -u

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [èːɾʉ]
    Rhymes: -èːɾʉ

Adjective[edit]

eru

  1. scarred
    ja jer ɑlldes eru oppe øgöm
    I'm completely scarred on my face

Derived terms[edit]

  • freikeneru (“freckled”)
