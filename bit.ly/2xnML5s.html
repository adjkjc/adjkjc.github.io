Protoplast (religion)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
  (Redirected from List of first men or women in mythology and religion)
Jump to navigation Jump to search
Not to be confused with protoplasts, the primitive cells or parts of a cell.

A protoplast, from ancient Greek πρωτόπλαστος (prōtóplastos, "first-formed"), in a religious context initially referred to the first human[1] or, more generally, to the first organized body of progenitors of mankind in a creation story (as in Manu and Shatrupa or Adam and Eve), or of surviving humanity after a cataclysm (as in Deucalion or Noah).

  • Kidili

Abrahamic religions
  • Adam and Eve
  • Noah
  • Adam Kadmon (esoteric)
  • Lilith (esoteric)

  • Kaliyan and Kalicchi

Aztec mythology

  • Oxomoco and Cipactonal

Baganda

Cherokee
  • Selu & Kanati

Cowichan peoples
  • Quiltumtun

Greek mythology

  • Deucalion and Pyrrha (the first modern humans)

Ho-Chunk
  • Kunu[citation needed]
  • An unnamed one-legged man[citation needed]
  • Turtle[citation needed]

Hinduism
  • Svayambhuva Manu and Shatarupa (first couple on earth)
  • Yama and Yamuna (first siblings on earth)
  • Including Sraddhadeva Manu and Shraddha (wife of Sraddhadeva Manu)

Inca mythology
  • Pacha Camac

Lakota people
  • Tokahe - first human emerged from the underworld
  • Wa and Ka

Maori mythology

Muisca mythology

Navajo mythology

Norse mythology

  • Líf and Lífþrasir (future)

Polynesian mythology
  • Ele'ele

  • Marikoriko and Tiki

  • Tonga
  • Vatea and Papa

Philippine mythology

Shintoism

  • Izanami

Traditional African religions
  • Kikuyu
    • Gikuyu and Mumbi
  • Serer creation myth

    • Jambooñ and Agaire (two sisters and early ancestors of the Serer and Jola people respectively whose pirogue broke at the Point of Sangomar separating the two groups[4][5][6])

    • Iya Nla

Turkic mythology
