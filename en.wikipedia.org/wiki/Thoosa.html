Thoosa

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search
This article is about the nymph. For the sponge genus, see Thoosa (sponge).

In Greek mythology, Thoosa (/θoʊˈoʊsə/, Greek: Θόωσα, romanized: Thóōsa), also spelled Thoösa, was, according to Homer, the nymph daughter of the primordial sea god Phorcys, and the mother, by Poseidon, of the Cyclops Polyphemus.[1]

Notes[edit]

  1. ^ Homer, Odyssey, 1.70–73. Heubeck, Hainsworth and West, p. 69 on line 71-3, notes that "Thoosa seems to be an ad hoc invention, her name recalling the swift movement of the waves".
