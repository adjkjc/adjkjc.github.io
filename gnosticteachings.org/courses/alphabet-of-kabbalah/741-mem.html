This is a transcription of an audio lecture which you can download for free: Alphabet of Kabbalah Course .

Mem מ is the thirteenth letter of the Kabbalistic Hebrew alphabet. It is also the second of the five final letters that when at the end of a Hebrew word are depicted differently.

You can see the two shapes of the letter in the spelling of the word מם Mem. On the right, the first shape is open at the bottom, it has a gap. This gap symbolizes the flowing wave made by the י Iod through the ray of creation, the ו Vav. The closed shape on the left also represents creation, but in potentiality, not in activity.

If we observe the shape of both Mem letters, we can visualize two previous letters. On the right side of the letter, we see כ Kaf, the eleventh Kabbalistic letter. In a previous lecture we talked very extensively about the letter כ Kaf. On the left side of the shape of Mem is the letter ו Vav, which is the sixth letter of the Kabbalistic alphabet. So, the letter י Iod, which is the head of the letter Vav is resting on the top of the letter Kaf. The bottom of the open letter מ Mem is made by the base of Vav and Kaf, but Kaf and Vav are closed in the ם final Mem, since the letter Vav is completely attached to the letter Kaf.

Mem is indeed a very profound letter, because it relates to creation and Kabbalistically to many profound things or words that we are going to teach today.

יום

To begin, let us see how the letter itself explains the Hebrew word יום yom, which means "day."

The letter Vav is made by extending its head, the letter Iod. We always find the letter י Iod as a small wave at the very top left of the letter Mem. Iod symbolizes Kether. Iod is the tenth letter of the Hebrew alphabet and is the first letter of יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei, the sacred name of God. So it is very important to see that the letter י Iod, as a small wave, is precisely at the top left in both variations of the letter Mem.

Let us observe the second stroke of the open letter Mem מ, which is made by the letter ו Vav or by the letter Iod, which when stretched reaches the bottom to even the first stroke or letter כ Kaf – from that second stroke of the opened Mem we take two letters, the י Iod and the ו Vav.

Thus י Iod ו Vav ם final Mem, spell the word יום yom, which means "day" in Hebrew. In the book of Genesis we find the word יום yom in all the days of creation, namely, the first יום yom, the second יום yom, that is, the first day, the second day, etc...

The י Iod plus the final Mem spells another word, ים yam, a word similar to יום yom. ים Yam means "sea, ocean." This is because both letters םמ Mem together with the letter י Iod in the middle, spell the word מים mayim, water.

So in the shape of both open Mem and final Mem, we see that the letter Iod is like a wave at the top left, which expresses that the Iod, which is the beginning of יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei, as a water wave, makes poosible the flowing of the Logos, the word. That is why in Kabbalah the letter Mem symbolizes water.

The letter Mem is one of the three mother letters that we always address in Kabbalah, namely Aleph, Shin, and Mem. We talked about Aleph in our first lecture. Now we address Mem, the symbol of water, which is always placed at the sephirah Yesod, the ninth sephirah of the Tree of Life. In the sephirah Yesod, we find the sexual energy.

Yam, or sea, is a symbol of the Absolute. Remember that above the Tree of Life, there are three aspects of the Absolute, namely, the Ain, the Ain Soph, and the Ain Soph Aur. The letter ם final Mem is related with the second aspect, Ain Soph. In other words, the word soph, which means "end, final," relates to the letter ם final Mem. We can say that it relates to the Unmanifested Mother. This is why in Gnostic esotericism we call ם final Mem "the Unmanifested Mother," the Soph, the end, the limitless; so Ain Soph is related to the ם final Mem, as we see it in the word ים, which means sea, which is where Iod (Kether) joins with the unknowable divine, the abstract absolute space, the final Mem; it is where the uncreated light ends and begins, the Unmanifested Mother, which I repeat is called Soph: the Ain Soph.

The Ain Soph also relates to the letter ה Hei of the holy name יהוה. Many times we have explained that the first explanation of the sacred name of God, which is יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei, that is translated in the Bible as Jehovah, relates to the three first Sephiroth of the Tree of Life: Kether, Chokmah, Binah—Iod Hei Vav—and the second ה Hei is placed in the Absolute, the Ain Soph, which is that feminine aspect, the unknowable feminine aspect, from where all the sephiroth emanate.

So יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei are Kether, Chokmah, Binah, and Ain Soph. יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei is the one Light, God, Yehidah, the Tetragrammaton that expresses through Kether, which in the shape of the letter Mem is represented by the letter כ Kaf, because the spelling of Kether begins with כ Kaf. Thus כ Kaf in the letter Mem represents the three primary forces—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—plus Ain Soph, the unknowable divine, which is the second ה Hei in יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei. All of this is synthesized in the letter Mem. As you see, it is easy to Kabbalistically grasp it and see it, because it is Kabbalistically hidden.

The letter כ Kaf on the right of the shape of מ Mem has, as we know, a value of 20. The letter ו Vav, on the left of the shape of Mem, has the value of 6. If we make the addition, we get 26, which is also the same value of יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei, because Iod is 10, Hei is 5, Vav is 6, and Hei again is 5, so you get 26, so it has the same value as the sacred name of God. This is why the letter final Mem hides the unmanifested and manifested God within.

The unmanifested ם final Mem, which is the Soph Mem or Mem Sophit ("final Mem"), is related to two of the three aspects of the Absolute: Ain Soph and Ain Soph Aur. The aspect we are addressing here is the second of the three, the Ain Soph.

The letter Mem is used to write the words Maya and Om.

In the Mayan bible, the Popol Vuh, we find a beautiful explanation of how the sea of life, in its abstract aspect, is described. The bible of the Mayans states:

"This is the account of how the wholeness was in suspense, in calm, in silence; motionless wholeness, still, and empty the vastness of space. This is the first account, the first narrative. There was neither man, nor animal, birds, fishes, crabs, trees, stones, caves, ravines, grasses, nor forests; there was only the broad vastness of space. There, the surface of the earth had not appeared. There, were only the calm sea and the great vastness of space. There, nothing was brought together, nothing that could make a sound, nor anything that might move, or tremble, or that could hum in space. There, nothing was standing; only the calm water, the placid, solitary, and tranquil sea: Nothing existed. There was only immobility and silence in the darkness, in the night. Only the Creator, the Maker, Tepeu, Gucumatz, the Forefathers, were in the water, surrounded by light. They were hidden under green and blue feathers, and were therefore called Gucumatz; innately great sages, great thinkers. In this manner space existed, and also the heart of Heaven, which is the name of God, and thus is how He is called. - Popol Vuh (The Mayan Bible)

This beautiful thought of the Mayan masters is a description of the Ain Soph together with those elements that we call יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei, but in repose, ready to begin their activity. So in other words, this is how the Mayans describe the universe emerging from the womb of the Divine Mother.

The Letter Mem and the Divine Mother

That womb is the unknowable, and this is why the letter Mem symbolizes the womb. The מ open Mem, which has a gap in the bottom, is the womb that is ready for parturition; it is that womb from which all the days of Genesis emerge, except the first. The first day is related with the ם final Mem, which is closed, represented in the word בראשית Bereshith (Genesis).

We stated previously that Bereshith is an anagram, and here we are going to explain it in relation with the letter Mem, in order to see how profound this Kabbalistic Genesis, the book of the Gnostics, is.￼

As you remember from the lecture on כ Kaf, we explained that בראשית Bereshith encloses the word ראש resh ("head"). The letter ר Resh represents a head. The letter ב Beth represents a house. So the head of God, as illustrated in the graphic, is Adam Kadmon Shani, which is the symbol of the head of God, which is the letter Iod that appears as Kether, the first sephirah of the Tree of Life. That head, spot, or Iod emerges from the Hei, the unknowable, because the Hei as the unknowable feminine aspect is the source of Kether.

Now, when Kether, the Iod, appears—which is that head—within that head the Hei is hidden. That Hei is the first Hei of יהוה Iod-Hei-Vav-Hei, and appears as his breath in order to create through the Logos, the Word. Their creation is the universe, represented in the letter Vav. So, the one that shapes the universe is always the unknowable and knowable Hei, the Mother.

￼ ￼

The Father is also a creator, but the Father only gives the י Iod, the seed. The Mother shapes it for nine months. We will say in Kabbalistic terms that the י Iod, the Father, which is Kether, gives the seed, and with it the mother חוה Chavah (Eve) creates the other nine Sephiroth, the Tree of Life חיים. That is why the Divine Mother is hidden; she becomes visible as the daughter of that head Adam Kadmon Shani. As you see in the graphic, we see the Atik Yomin, the Ancient of Ancients, seated on his throne. Emerging from his head the Shechinah, his "daughter"; the Divine Mother is that Shechinah, the 'Hei' but in a visible aspect.

In Greek and Roman mythology, we also find this myth, as Athena / Minerva, the daughter of Zeus / Jupiter. She was born from his head.

In Kabbalah, in the Zohar, the Shechinah on the head of the ancient of days is another Iod, on top of the Iod. It forms the crown of the Ancient of Days. The crown of the Ancient of Days is Shechinah, the Divine Mother, who becomes Kether's daughter.

In the word בראשית Bereshith is the explanation of this process. The letter ב Beth at the beginning together with the letter ת Tav at the end of the word, make the word בת bath, which in Hebrew means "daughter." The letters between Beth and Tav are ראשי which spell the word rashi, meaning "first, chief, origin, prima," indicating the most important performer or role, the first. So if we relate this to Italian, we form prima donna, "the first lady," yet, in Hebrew we say, ראשי בת, "the first daughter." Do you see the beautiful symbol here?

Genesis 1:1 can be translated as:

בראשית ברא אלהים את השמים ואת הארץ

"The first daughter created Elohim , the heavens, and the earth."

Bereshith is the first word of Genesis. The second and third words are Bera Elohim.

That daughter coming from the head of the Ancient of Days, the Father, is the first visible feminine aspect. That feminine aspect is the Divine Mother always hidden in the three primary forces, represented in the letter א Aleph. She is "wisdom," Chokmah, the second Sephirah, the Son, which corresponds to the second letter ה 'Hei' of the name of God יהוה. She is in that ה Hei within Chokmah, as well as in the Iod in the head of Kether, which is the Iod of יהוה Iod-Hei Vav-Hei. So, She manifests herself in all the Sephiroth in different ways. She is also at the end of יהוה Iod-Hei Vav-Hei, because we know that the last Hei is also the Unknowable She.

אימה

Aleph, Iod, Mem, and Hei spell אימה aima, "mother." אימה Aima is Aima Elohim or Akash, Aima Elah-Yam, "the Sea Goddess Mother."

This universe is represented in the shape of the letter א Aleph, which as we explained previously represents the three sephiroth Kether, Chokmah, Binah, which relate to the worlds of Atziluth, Briah and Yetzirah. All of this manifested existence (א) comes from י Iod, מ Mem, and the ה Hei, the Ain Soph. Thus: אימה: all of the universe comes from the Divine Mother.

The Iod is hidden in the shape of the letter מ Mem, at the top left. The two letters together form the word ים yam, which means "sea." ים yam is at the end of אלהים Elohim, which is the word that means "Gods and Goddesses."

"The waters (in Ain) above the firmament (Atziluth), the male part ה ים Ha-Ym; the waters below (in Assiah and in the Ain Soph), the female part אימה Yama; those above were designated אלהים Elohim (and Aelohim), and distinguished by the first ה Hei in the divine name יהוה; those below (in Assiah and in the Ain Soph) were called אדני ה Adonia, and characterized by the second ה Hei." – Liber Adami

By following the explanation of Genesis, related with the letter Mem, we find that ראשי בת Bath Rashi, the Prima Dona, the first lady, the daughter of the Abstract Absolute Space, the letter ה Hei, created אלה Elah or Eloha: Remember that the Divine Mother expresses herself in different aspects, so אלה Elah is another aspect of the Divine Mother. אלה Elah is feminine. The Iod and the Mem at the end of אלהים Elohim or אלה-ים Elah Im means sea, so אלה-ים Elah Im means "the Sea Goddess" or the feminine sea: This is hidden in the word Elohim, which means "Gods and Goddesses."

Bereshith ראשי בת Bath Rashi, ים-אלה ברא Bera Elah-Yam can mean "The first daughter created the Sea Goddess in the heavens and the earth."

In Hebrew, the heavens is השמים את At ha shamayim. את At (Aleph and Tav) means "the," yet it also means "you," but feminine. So, in other words, the first daughter created Elah-Yam ים-אלה את At, which is also feminine. השמים Hah Shamayim, the letter ה Hei is also "She."

These are precisely the manifestation of the feminine aspects of divinity. First Bereshith, the Prima Donna, the first lady, the first daughter, ראשי בת Bath Rashi created Elah-Yam At את ים-אלה. This means that אלה-ים the first feminine sea, or Sea Goddess, manifests through the Word, the first and the last letters of the Kabbalistic Alphabet את At, which also means "She." Likewise, the Hei in השמים the heavens, is feminine, you see? She manifests the Word in 'the heavens' because את At means "you," but feminine.

"You" את, Mother, as a Logos, Word, manifests through the ה Hei, the breath, which is the first letter of השמים, "the heavens," which in Hebrew is את השמים At Ha-Shamayim.

...And the Earth

Thereafter, it is written in Hebrew ואת הארץ Ve-At Ha-Arets, which is usually translated to mean "and the earth." Yet we can see that it means "And you [Divine Mother] manifest the Word through the Hei [the breath], in the Earth." The Earth is the sephirah מלכות Malkuth, which begins with מ Mem. The Earth is הארץ Ha-Arets in Hebrew. The sephirah Malkuth is the Earth, and is precisely where She resides; the Divine Mother resides in the whole of nature. Thus the whole phrase:

בראשית ברא אלהים את השמים ואת הארץ

"The first daughter created Elohim, the heavens, and the earth."

This is usually translated as "In the beginning God created the Heavens and the Earth. But really, this first line of the Bible is an complete explanation of how the Divine Mother emerges from the unknowable, and appears as the daughter on the head of God, and creates Elohim, or the Feminine aspect (the Mem) in Heaven and Earth. In other words, She creates through the letter ם Mem in Heaven and through the other letter מ Mem in Earth. This is hidden in that phrase.

Following the narration of Genesis, the next line is,

והארץ היתה תהו

"And the Earth was formless and void."

This implies that יראש-תב Bath Rashi (the Prime Matter) which emerged from the unknowable divine, the ם final Mem, finally reaches Malkuth, yet its creative power is only in potentiality, since Malkuth, the earth, is formless and void. Everything is within the final Mem, formless and void, meaning, in chaos.

Malkuth represents our physical body, which is also that ם final Mem, yet visible, where everything is formless and void, because Malkuth is the final sephirah, the tenth, of all the emanations that come from the unknowable, from the very bosom or womb of the Divine Mother. We stated that Malkuth is feminine. So the whole physical body is feminine - that is why the physical body, Malkuth, in Kabbalah is called מצרים Mitzrahim. We find this word in the Bible translated as "Egypt." It begins with the מ open Mem, and ends with ם final Mem; the two Mems are there in the word Mizrahim.

So this is why we have to start working with צ Tzade, in order to transmute the נ Nun.

The Power of Creation

When we start doing alchemy, then we become a Tzadik or letter Tzade, and we take צ Tzade out from מצרים Mitzrahim, transforming מצרים Mizrahim into the word מרים Miriam - which also begins with the open Mem and ends with the final Mem. מרים Miriam is the Divine Mother, who in the Zohar is the Shechinah. מרים Miriam appears as a sister of משה Moses, whose name also begins with Mem. Miriam was the name of the Mother of Jesus of Nazareth, which is translated as Mary, in English, but in Hebrew is Miriam.

Miriam is Maria, that ocean of life, ocean of water, within which the bent נ Nun is hidden, which is the צ Tzadik, Christ symbolized by Jesus. So Jesus Christ symbolically speaking, the משיח Messiah, which begins with Mem as well, is (in Aramaic) מר יה Lord Jah in Chokmah, the son of מרים Miriam, מריה Maria: Do you see this beautiful symbol of Jesus of Nazareth and Mary? All of this, which unfortunately millions of Christians ignore.

It is impossible to create without the Mem, without the Mother. It is impossible to be alive without מים mayim, water.

As אלה-ים Elah-Yam, the Sea Goddess, that feminine sea, she said—or better said, אלהים Elohim said through אלה-ים Elah-Yam: "Let there be light!" The Union of the Two Mems

We can awaken the שכינה Shechinah inside of ourselves by the action of the two מם Mems - the Mem of the man and the Mem of the woman. The Latin M inverted is the W; it is the same M, but inverted. That is why in the sign of Aquarius, the Mem of the man is on top, and the W (the inverted M) is below. The W symbolizes the WO-man in English.￼

But why are we explaining this? Because together the letters M and W form the ancient Hebrew Mem! The above graphic shows how they wrote Mem in ancient times. It is like the M and W that we have in Latin, with which we write woman and man. In this way we can say that the Latin W corresponds to ם final Mem, and the letter M in Latin is the open מ Mem.￼

M and W together form the hieroglyphic of Aquarius, which comes from aqua (water) plus aerius (air, aerial).

When man and woman are united, they form an "Elohim," a combination of male and female that hovers over the waters of sexuality. Through the union of those two polarities (male and female), sexual energy is activated, enflamed, and becomes "light." That light is the שכינה Shechinah, the Divine Mother as light. "And there was light!" That light is the Divine Mother. If we continue talking about this in Genesis, we will see how all of the days of Genesis emerge from her, from that letter Mem.

The second day for instance says, "Let the waters be divided, the superior waters from the inferior waters, in order for the firmament to appear." That firmament emerged from her, because the superior waters are the מ Mem above, that are pouring into the ם Mem below. The מ Mem above is שמים Schamayim, "the Heavens." The ם Mem below is in Malkuth, "the earth."

As we explained in the lecture about א the letter Aleph, the two waters are united in the letter א Aleph, but here in the letter Mem they separate. So when it is written, "And Elohim said, 'Let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters," it should say, "Let there be a firmament between the two letters Mem." As well, "let there be a firmament in the womb of the mother, and let us separate מ the superior Mem from ם the inferior Mem - the מ open Mem from the ם Mem Sophit or final Mem - so that the firmament will appear."

Meaning, "Let the superior agree with the inferior and the superior agree with the inferior," to make the miracle of one thing. This seal encloses a marvelous work that we should perform in ourselves.

Those twelve constellations are from the twelve powers of the Divine Mother Space. All of these powers are within us. Parts of them, as archetypes, descend into the physical body. This is why we are born in a different zodiacal sign in every life in accordance to the law of reincarnation. So the twelve archetypes, or the twelve tribes of Israel, are within us. By accomplishing the transmutation of the superior and inferior waters, we unite the twelve and twelve, and then we find the twenty-fourth Arcanum, The Weaver, which is the Divine Mother uniting the waters from above and below in order to "perform the miracle of one thing," the creation of her Son, Adam. That is the mystery, yet it is more profound.

Audience: In the book Revolutionary Psychology, Samael Aun Weor said, "Who are we?" Does this refer to 'Me and Asher' which is 'Who' in Kabbalah?

Instructor: All the books of Master Samael Aun Weor are 100% Kabbalistic and he said not to read his books like a newspaper. "Who are we?" he says in Revolutionary Psychology. He is asking us, and we are answering "We are Who (מי me)." "Who" is the Real Being within us, Binah, Mercury, because Binah is the God Mercury, Shiva, the first begotten of Space, our own particular Monad. He is "Who." So who are we? We are Who, but we are saying it only intellectually. The main thing is to discover that consciously.

The Mayans say, "Who are we?" Then they say, "Are we that which we are? Am I what I am?"

The question is: "Are you what you are?" If Who (מי me) is Binah, the Holy Spirit, who are we? Our real Being is God, the Holy Spirit. Are we that? No! That is why the Mayan Kabbalists say, "Am I the one that I am?" Do you see the question here? This is very profound.

The answer, if you honestly answer it, is No! We are not Who! We have to reach the thirteenth commandment; we have to die in our many selves in order to become what I am. Because "I am" is: Eheieh Asher Eheieh, the Monad, and if we accomplish Self-realization, then we will be That. Before that we are not, yet we are part of it.

Remember, the Divine Mother is with us, even if she is not awakened yet, she is with us. We have to awake her. When we pray to our Divine Mother, "Please, Mother of mine, help me with this and that," you are talking to that divine aspect, which is called Elah-Yam. But she wants to be IN you. When you awaken her inside yourself, then she rises inside your spinal column as שכינה Shechinah, then she is within you. Then we are happy, because we find Abraham and Sarah united, or Isaac and Rebecca united, or Jacob and Rachel. The Divine Mother there inside of you is the Kundalini, the שכינה Shechinah in different levels in accordance with Initiation, so we have to develop the Divine Mother within us: Mem.

Audience: In some traditions the number thirteen is bad luck; can you expose more about it?

Instructor: Well, the number thirteen is bad luck for the ego, but good luck for the Being, that's for sure! Friday the 13th is a tradition in English. Friday is Freya, the Goddess of Love, the Divine Mother in The Edda, the Nordic myth, from there comes the word "Friday." So, Friday the 13th is related with Mem, mavet, death. Everyone is scared of death. Everyone is scared of dying, right? We have to be happy to die. We have to welcome death. We need to die. "My beloved Mother Death, come to me, kill me." She only kills the devils that are inside you. She cannot kill your soul. She cannot destroy your Being, because those elements are immortal. Your egos are not immortal, so she can destroy them, so invite her on Good Friday, the thirteenth, the beginning of Shabbath, invite her to kill your ego.

If you do not want her to kill you here, then she will kill you in hell, because she does not abandon her son or daughter. In the eighth Dantesque circle, she becomes a viper in order to destroy that ego in order to liberate her son, her soul, her daughter from hell.

Audience: In South America it is Tuesday the 13th...

Instructor: In South America they say Tuesday the 13th, but it makes more sense as Friday the 13th, because Tuesday is related with Thor. It might also have the same sense, since Thor is the Holy Spirit, Binah, from which the thunder comes.

Audience: Should we be looking at the books in Spanish in order to understand the books more Kabbalistically?

Instructor: Whether it is in Spanish, English, Chinese, French, Portuguese or any other language, when we say "Who are we?" it is the same question.

In Kabbalah, מי me is "who." That מי me is the Holy Spirit, the reality within us. Shiva, Mercury, the first begotten; the Monad – that is the reality within us. That is מי me in Kabbalah. Thus, whether we interpret the Kabbalah in Spanish or in Portuguese or any other language, we always have to go to the source of the bible, which is written in Hebrew. Understand?

Audience: What would be a good practice to do for Mother's Day?

Instructor: Tomorrow is Mother's Day, the tenth of May. You think it is a coincidence? Tomorrow is the tenth. What is tomorrow? 10, IO, the I is the phallus, the man, and the O is the uterus, the woman. As you see, the letter ם final Mem has the shape of the letter O. And the letter Iod which is on the top left is the letter I. IO. IO is Mem.

May is the month of the mother. Tomorrow is Mother's Day. Kabbalistically, it is a very beautiful day. It is the only modern holiday that I see is placed in the right place from a Kabbalistic point of view.

The Divine Mother is in all the practices. She is in Sexual Magic and Pranayama. She is everywhere. If you want to find a practice where the Divine Mother is not there, then that practice is not good. She has to be present in all practices. In all the practices that we do, she is there, because she is the Light of God. Not even God can exist without the Mother. He, God, masculine, emerged from the womb of Mother Space, which is Soph in Hebrew, the שכינה Shechinah unmanifested, and from the head emanates the daughter, which is She. So, any spiritual practice is good.

Remember Her, love Her, and remember that She is always within you. That is the best practice, to give all your love to Her.

Honor your Mother and your Father who are within you.

Practice the mantra ISIS, pronounced,

IIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIII SSSSSSSSSSSSSS IIIIIIIIIII SSSSSSSSSSSS

Put your arms straight above your head, making the Latin letter "I" with your body. Point your fingers to heaven, straight, and in that position pronounce ISIS. This is a simple practice that you can perform every day.

Have a happy Mother's Day.
