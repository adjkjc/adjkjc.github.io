New unemployment claims in Massachusetts still below 50K in week before state began reopening from coronavirus shutdowns

Updated May 21, 10:29 AM; Posted May 21, 8:43 AM
By Jim Kinney | jkinney@repub.com

The number of new unemployment claims in Massachusetts fell again last week but remained above historic levels as the state prepared to enter the first phase of reopening from shutdowns related to the coronavirus pandemic.

The state saw 38,328 new claims for the week that ended May 16, the federal government said Thursday. It was the second straight week the number of new claims was below 50,000 after spiking to nearly 200,000 in the last week of March, according to data from the U.S. Employment and Training Administration.

The latest numbers come one day before the state releases its April unemployment rate. This will be the first time the rate reflects the impact of the COVID-19 crisis because it will be based on surveys of workplaces and of households conducted in April. The rate released in April was based on March surveys completed before widespread shutdowns.

Since mid-March, nearly 865,000 Massachusetts workers have filed applications for unemployment benefits, with several weeks seeing the number of new claims soar well above 100,000.

Between January 2000 and early March 2020, Massachusetts had never recorded more than 30,000 new claims in a single week.

Nationally, meanwhile, 2.4 million Americans applied for unemployment last week, bringing the total to more than 38 million since the crisis began.

U.S. gross domestic product shrank at a an annual rate of 4.8% in the first quarter, and numbers for the second quarter are expected to be even worse.

The Massachusetts real gross domestic product declined by 6.1% in the first quarter of 2020, according to MassBenchmarks.

This week the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston predicted double-digit unemployment rates until the end of calendar year 2020.
