Up, Up, And Away

Our Journey

 
 

 

–     2020

Imgur launches Imgur Emerald, a subscription product that offers users an ad-free experience and exclusive perks.

Imgur introduces a new post page experience with improvements to video scaling, the scroll bar, and engagement features. 

–     2019

Imgur celebrates its 10th birthday and reaches 300 million people per month.

Imgur launches Melee, a community-powered app to discover and talk about the best in gaming. 

Imgur raises $20M from Coil to change the way content is monetized. 

–     2018

Imgur now supports video uploads.

Imgur is listed as one of the “100 websites that shaped the internet as we know it” by Gizmodo. 

–     2017

Imgur now reaches 250 million people per month.

Imgur turns users into curators with Favorite Folders. 

Imgur is among the top 10 websites to drive comments to the FCC in support of net neutrality.

 

–     2016

Imgur launches messaging on iOS, Android, and web.

Imgur wins AdWeek's Readers Choice for Hottest Social Platform.

Imgur launches new content creation and curation experiences.

 

–     2015

Imgur reaches 150 million people per month.

Imgur wins 2015 Webby awards for Best Social Media and Best Community Website.

 

–     2014

Imgur raises $40 million from Andreessen Horowitz, its first external investment.

 

–     2013

Imgur crosses 100 million monthly active users.

Imgur wins "Best Bootstrapped Startup" at the TechCrunch "Crunchies" Awards for the second time.

 

–     2012

Imgur launches User Submitted, giving Imgurians new ways to share images on Imgur, explore and curate content.

 

–     2011

Imgur wins "Best Bootstrapped Startup" at the TechCrunch "Crunchies" Awards.

 

–     2010

Imgur launches the Gallery, which organizes and showcases the most popular visual stories on Imgur.
 

–     2009

Alan Schaaf creates and launches Imgur in his Ohio University dorm room as a simple, no-limits platform to shares images online.
